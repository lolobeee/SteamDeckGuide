6. The Steam Store

6.1 Accessing the Steam Store

To access the Steam Store, press the Steam Button and select Store.


Figure 6.1: The Store option within the Steam Menu.

6.2 How to Buy a Game

To buy a game on the Steam Deck, go to the Steam Store.

When you go to the Steam Store, it brings you first to a page called Great on Deck. The Great on Deck page within the Steam Store shows only games that are Verified. For more about the Verified status and Steam Deck compatibility, please go to Section 5.1 Verified Status.


Figure 6.2: The Great on Deck page of the Steam Store

Within this page, it also shows the games that are currently popular to play on the Steam Deck.

The rest of the page is split into different Categories of games (for example, Action, Casual, RPG, etc.)

Find a game you wish to purchase either from browsing the categories or using the Search Steam section of the Store (for more on how to do that, see Section 6.6 Searching the Steam Store.)

On a game's Store Page, you can see its Verified status at a glance under the Steam Deck Compatability heading. You can also click the Learn More button to see why it has been given this status.

Under a game's Store Page banner, you can see more details about the game such as publisher, publish date, etc.

To purchase the game, simply click on the Add to Cart button.


Figure 6.3: The Store Page for "Sea of Stars."

You can keep shopping or press View My Cart to finishing checking out.

Once purchased, you will be given the option to install your game right away. If you do not install right away and want to install later, you can do so by finding your game within your Steam Library and selecting Install. For more on this, see Section 5.2 Installing Games.

6.3 Store Home

The Store Home page of the Steam Deck is very similar to the Steam Store on the Desktop version of Steam.

Here, you can browse all games, not just those that are Verified.

You can see what games are Featured and Recommended and can browse by Category.

There are Community Recommendations, games your friends are playing, games on sale, and much more.


Figure 6.4: Steam Store Home

6.4 Categories

The next page over from the Store Home is Categories. Here, you can browse games by their category.

It is sorted first by large Categories, like Action, Adventure, etc. Then, more specifically within that Category. For example, the Category of First-Person Shooter is within the Action Category.


Figure 6.5: Categories page

6.5 Wishlist

The Wishlist page is where all games that you have selected to put on your Wishlist will appear.


Figure 6.6: The Wishlist page.

To put a game onto your Wishlist, find its page within the Steam Store (see the next section, Section 6.6 Searching the Steam Store for help on how to find games.)

Scroll down in the game's page until you find the Add to Your Wishlist button and select it. Now, that game will appear in your Wishlist, allowing you to get notifications about when it goes on sale, or when it is released if it is not out yet.


Figure 6.7: The Add to Your Wishlist button.

If you do not want to add a game to your Wishlist, but still get notified about any updates, you can select the Follow button. Alternatively, if you do not want this game to show up on your Recommended section of the Steam Store, you can select the Ignore button. The Flag button allows you to report a game, if you believe something about it is illegal, offensive, etc.

6.6 Searching the Steam Store

To search for games within the Steam Store, go to the Search Steam page of the store.


Figure 6.8: The Search Steam page.

From there, you can type in keywords for your search.

Steam will list titles based on the Sort By criteria as well as your preferences.


Figure 6.9: An example of a search within the Steam Store for relevance to the phrase, "cats."

For each game listed in your search results, Steam will show you its price, release date, and overall rating based on reviews in the Steam Store:

  • indicates positive reviews
  • indicates mixed reviews
  • indicates negative reviews

You can also see if the games in your results are Verified based on their icon. For more information about Verified status, see the earlier section, Section 5.1 Verified Status.

Any games that you already own will show up with this symbol: to indicate that they are already in your library.

You can sort your search by other criteria besides relevancy by clicking the drop-down list next to Sort By. You can sort by:

  • Release Date
  • Name
  • Lowest Price
  • Highest Price
  • User Reviews
  • Steam Deck Compatibility Review Date

Figure 6.10: The Sort By menu.

6.7: How to Write a Review

To write a Review for a game, go to the game's Page either by:

  • Finding the game within your Steam Library and going to the Game Info section and clicking on Steam Store
  • Searching for the game on the Steam Store

Figure 6.11: Getting to the Store Page from the Game Page

Scroll down until you see the dialogue box with the title, "Write a review for [game name]."

After typing in your review into the dialogue box, you can choose to have your review show up as Public or Friends Only by clicking on the Settings button under the dialogue box.

You can indicate whether or not you recommend the game to others by clicking on the appropriate button.

When you are ready to post your review, click the Post Review button!


Figure 6.12: writing a review for the game "Among Us" on the Steam Store.
