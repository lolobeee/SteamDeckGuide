2. Controls

2.1 Controls Overview

The main controls of the Steam Deck are the Thumbsticks, Trackpads, and ABXY buttons.

These are all located on the front of the Steam Deck.


Figure 1: Front view of the Steam Deck controls. Image taken from https://www.steamdeck.com/en/tech/deck.

To navigate within the menus on the Steam Deck, you can use any of the following:

  • Left Thumbstick
  • D-Pad
  • Left Trackpad
  • Touchscreen

To select, press the A Button.

To unselect, or go back, press the B Button.

To press L3 or R3, you press down on their respective Thumbstick.

The Menu, Steam, and View buttons all are primarily used for bringing up menus. The Menu and View buttons are also sometimes referred to as "Start" and "Select." For more about the Steam button and the "Steam Menu", see Section 4: The Steam Menu.

The Quick Access button can be used to quickly change settings such as brightness and audio. For more on this, see Section 10: Quick Access Menu

The Steam Deck's speakers are located on its front, as well as two microphones located above the 7" touchscreen.

Other commonly used buttons include the R1, R2, L1, and L2 buttons. These are located on the top sides of the Steam Deck and are known as "trigger" buttons. Within menus, they can generally be used for switching between menu pages and flipping through lists more quickly.

Every game will have different control bindings. To learn more about how to change these, see Section 5.3: "Game Menu".


Figure 2: Top view of the Steam Deck's controls. Image taken from https://www.steamdeck.com/en/tech/deck.

The Power button is located at the top of the Steam Deck. It can be used to put your Steam Deck into sleep mode. For more on how to shut your Steam Deck down completely, see Section 4.9: Power.

The Steam Deck uses a 45W USB-C charger, and its charging port is located at the top of the Steam Deck near the Power button.

The Volume buttons are located at the top of the Steam Deck. The Steam Deck has a headphone jack for plugging in headphones. It also allows for Bluetooth headphones. For more on Bluetooth, see Section 11.TBD Bluetooth.

On the back of the Steam Deck, there is a microSD card slot. For more information about how to add a microSD card to your Steam Deck for more memory, please see this guide from Polygon.com: "How to Install and Format an SD Card on Steam Deck".


Figure 3: Back view of the Steam Deck controls. Image taken from https://www.steamdeck.com/en/tech/deck.

Some games will require the use of the four buttons on the back known as R4, R5, L4, and L5. These are flat, square buttons that act similarly to the other trigger buttons.

When prompted to type in information, the "Keyboard Screen" will appear.

You can type by using the Left Thumbstick, D-Pad, or the Right Trackpad to navigate and A to select a key. Alternatively, you can use the touchscreen to select keys.

There are several shortcuts that will be displayed on the keyboard (for example, X can be used for Backspace.)


Figure 4: The Keyboard Screen.

For a more in-depth look at the Steam Deck's controls, here is a helpful guide on the Steam Community by "Chronos": Steam Deck Controller Guide: a Visual Introduction.


2.2 Button Shortcuts

There are several helpful button shortcuts you can perform:

Button Shortcut Action
Steam + B (long press) Force Game Shutdown
Steam + X (release press) Show Keyboard Screen
Steam + R1 Take Screenshot
Steam + L2 Right Mouse Click
Steam (hold) + move Right Thumbstick Mouse Cursor Mode
Steam + R2 Left Mouse Click
Steam + L2 Right Mouse Click
Steam + Left Thumbstick (pointed up) Brightness Up
Steam + Left Thumbstick (pointed down) Brightness Down

For a complete list of shortcuts, press down the Steam button for a few moments to bring up the list on screen.
